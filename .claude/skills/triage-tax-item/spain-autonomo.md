# Sub-recipe: Spain-autonomo

For AEAT (Agencia Tributaria) notices and modelo filing obligations tied to
the Spanish SL / autónomo activity. Documents are frequently in Spanish —
extract and translate the key facts, don't just paraphrase the letter.

## How to read it

- Identify whether it's (a) a routine filing deadline/reminder for a modelo
  the user files periodically, or (b) an actual notification/requerimiento
  from AEAT about something specific (audit, discrepancy, missing filing).
- Look for the document type in the header: "Notificación", "Diligencia",
  "Requerimiento", "Comunicación", "Propuesta de liquidación",
  "Resolución", vs. just a modelo/calendar reminder.
- Look for a "plazo" (deadline) stated in the body — often "en el plazo de
  10 días hábiles" (10 business days) from notification, not from the
  letter's date.

## Common modelo filings (routine, not a notice from AEAT)

| Modelo | What it is | Standard deadline window |
|---|---|---|
| 130 | Quarterly IRPF (income tax) payment on account, autónomo | 1–20 Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct |
| 303 | Quarterly IVA (VAT) return | 1–20 Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct |
| 349 | Quarterly EU intra-community operations declaration | 1–20 of month after quarter (if applicable) |
| 111 | Quarterly withholding on payroll/professional invoices | 1–20 Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct |
| 390 | Annual VAT summary | Jan (with Q4's 303) |
| 100 | Annual Renta (income tax return) | Apr–Jun |
| 200 | Annual Impuesto de Sociedades (SL corporate tax) | 25 days after 6 months post fiscal year-end (typically Jul for calendar-year SLs) |
| 036/037 | Census registration/modification | Whenever activity/data changes — no fixed calendar slot |

If the document is just a calendar reminder for one of these, urgency is
🟢/🟡 based on how close the window is, and next action is "file/pay via
gestor or Sede Electrónica before the window closes."

## Actual AEAT notices — urgency rules

- **Notificación via DEHú (Dirección Electrónica Habilitada)**: 🔴 Critical
  regardless of stated topic — Spanish electronic-notification rules deem
  the notice accepted (and the clock started) after 10 calendar days of
  non-access, even if the user never opens it. Flag this explicitly.
- **Requerimiento / Propuesta de liquidación** (a specific ask for
  documents, or a proposed additional-tax assessment): 🔴 Critical if it
  states a "plazo de alegaciones" (objection window), since missing it
  waives the right to respond.
- **Diligencia** from an in-progress inspection: 🟡 Moderate–🔴 Critical
  depending on whether it sets a deadline or is just recording a step.
- **Comunicación informativa** with no requested action: 🟢 Low.

## Next action patterns

- Routine modelo: "confirm figures with gestor and file before the window
  closes."
- Requerimiento/liquidación: "have the gestor/asesor fiscal review the
  notice and prepare a response (pay, appeal, or provide the requested
  documents) before the plazo."
- DEHú notification not yet opened: "open and read the notification
  immediately — the response clock is running regardless."

## Documents/info typically needed

- NIF/CIF and the SL's registered name as shown on the notice.
- The notice/document itself, in full.
- Prior modelo filings for the period referenced.
- Invoices/facturas for the quarter or year in question.
- Gestor/asesor fiscal contact, since most responses are filed through them.
