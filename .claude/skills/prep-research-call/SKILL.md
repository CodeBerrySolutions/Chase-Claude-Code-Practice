# Prep Research Call

Produce a call brief for a research call with a coach prospect. Berry Nova is pre-launch and selling founding-client spots through direct conversations. This call's only job is to LOCATE DEMAND and gather signal — not to pitch, demo, or close. Write the brief so Phil walks in ready to listen.

## Inputs

Gather everything provided: prospect name, prior notes, prior outreach messages, links, transcripts. If prior notes or an outreach message exist, incorporate them — never write a question that re-asks something already known; instead write a follow-up that builds on it (e.g. known: "runs group program" → ask how the group tier changes their delivery load, not "what do you offer?").

If the input names a prospect but gives no context, say so in the Snapshot ("No prior notes — confirm basics early") and keep the rapport questions broader.

## Output Structure

Produce the brief in exactly this order. Keep the whole thing scannable in under 2 minutes — it is read right before the call. Use short lines, bold lead-ins, no paragraphs longer than 2 lines.

### 1. Snapshot (3-4 lines)

- Who they are and their niche.
- Their methodology (named framework? signature process?).
- Delivery model: 1:1, group, cohort, async, hybrid.
- Their door, if known: **acquisition-mode** (hunting for clients) vs **overloaded** (throttling demand). Mark "unknown — determine on call" if unclear.

### 2. Probe Areas (three mandatory)

**a. Capacity ceiling.** Are they truly at or near capacity? What breaks first if they take one more client — their calendar, their delivery quality, their evenings? Probe for specifics, not vibes: current client count, waitlist, hours per client per week.

**b. Methodology documentation.** Does written or audio documentation of their method exist? How complete? Could someone learn the important parts from audio alone? This is a hard ICP gate — Berry Nova clones documented methodology. No documentation, no fit (yet). Probe: course materials, SOPs, recorded calls, frameworks written down vs living only in their head.

**c. Demand signal.** If Berry Nova built for them first, what would they pull for? What is the first thing they would hand to an AI operator? Listen for the task they name unprompted — that is the demand. Do not suggest tasks; let them reach.

### 3. Listen-For Signals

List concrete cues, split into confirm and deny:

**Overloaded (confirms fit):**
- Mentions hiring or wanting a VA / assistant / junior coach.
- Turning clients away, waitlist, "not taking anyone new until…".
- Raised prices specifically to throttle demand.
- Complains about repetitive delivery work (check-ins, feedback, onboarding).

**Disqualifying (flag it):**
- "I need more clients" or any lead-gen hunger — flag as acquisition-mode, likely not ICP now.
- Methodology entirely improvised / "every client is different, I wing it".
- Nothing written down and no appetite to document.

### 4. Questions (5-8, open, ordered)

Order: rapport → operations → documentation → demand. Rules:
- Open questions only ("How do you…", "What happens when…", "Walk me through…").
- No leading questions ("Wouldn't it be great if…" is banned).
- Zero pitch language — no product names, features, or benefits inside questions.
- Skip anything already answered in prior notes; go one level deeper instead.

### 5. Traps (2-3)

Name what NOT to do on this call. Always include, adapted to the prospect:
- Do not pitch features or explain how the product works.
- Do not promise lead-gen, growth, or "more clients" — wrong door, wrong promise.
- Do not talk pricing or founding-client terms; if asked, defer: "Not selling anything today — I'm trying to understand how you work."

## Quality Bar

- Whole brief fits on one screen where possible; hard cap ~2-minute read.
- Every probe and question must be answerable by the coach talking about THEIR business, never by Phil explaining Berry Nova.
- If documentation status is already known from notes, shift probe (b) to depth/completeness questions instead of existence questions.

## Worked Example (condensed)

Input: "Call tomorrow with Dana Reyes, strength coach for postpartum moms, runs 'Rebuild Method' group program. Prior note: she tweeted about closing enrollment early last month."

> **SNAPSHOT** — Dana Reyes, postpartum strength coach. Named framework: Rebuild Method, delivered as group program with weekly form-check feedback. Door: likely **overloaded** (closed enrollment early last month — confirm why).
>
> **PROBES** — (a) Capacity: what forced the early close — client count, feedback load, calendar? What breaks first with 5 more members? (b) Documentation: is Rebuild Method written down or recorded — curriculum, videos, coaching notes? Could a new coach learn it from the materials alone? (c) Demand: which recurring task would she hand off first — form checks, check-in replies, onboarding?
>
> **LISTEN FOR** — Confirm: waitlist talk, VA/assistant mentions, price raised to slow signups, groans about form-check volume. Deny/flag: "trying to fill the next cohort" (acquisition-mode), "the method changes for every mom" with nothing written.
>
> **QUESTIONS** — 1. What led you to close enrollment early last month? 2. Walk me through a normal delivery week right now. 3. What eats the most hours per client? 4. How is Rebuild Method captured — what exists in writing or video? 5. If a new coach joined you tomorrow, how would they learn it? 6. What's the first task you'd hand to a capable assistant? 7. What have you already tried to get time back?
>
> **TRAPS** — Don't demo or describe the product. Don't hint at helping her "grow" — she's throttling, not hunting. If pricing comes up: "not selling today."

## Delivery

Return the brief as markdown in chat, ready to skim. Do not write it to a file unless asked.
